# SQL Analysis Results

## Overview

SQL was used to analyze employee attrition patterns across departments, job roles, overtime status, marital status, income levels, and employee tenure.

The analysis was conducted using SQLite on a dataset containing 1,470 employee records.

## Key Findings

### Overall Attrition

- Total Employees: 1,470
- Employees Who Left: 237
- Overall Attrition Rate: 16.1%

### Attrition by Department

- Sales: 20.6%
- Human Resources: 19.0%
- Research & Development: 13.8%

Sales had the highest departmental attrition rate.

### Attrition by Overtime

- Employees Working Overtime: 30.5%
- Employees Not Working Overtime: 10.4%

Employees working overtime experienced an attrition rate nearly three times higher than employees who did not work overtime.

### Attrition by Job Role

The highest attrition rates were:

- Sales Representative: 39.8%
- Laboratory Technician: 23.9%
- Human Resources: 23.1%
- Sales Executive: 17.5%
- Research Scientist: 16.1%

Sales Representatives had the highest attrition rate among all job roles.

### Attrition by Marital Status

- Single: 25.5%
- Married: 12.5%
- Divorced: 10.1%

Single employees experienced the highest attrition rate.

### Attrition by Monthly Income

Employees earning between $1,000 and $4,999 per month had an attrition rate of 21.8%, indicating higher attrition among lower-income employees.

### Attrition by Employee Tenure

Employees with 0–4 years at the company had an attrition rate of 24.3%.

This suggests that employee retention challenges are concentrated among employees earlier in their tenure.

### Combined Risk Factors

Employees who worked overtime and had 0–4 years of tenure experienced a 43.1% attrition rate.

This was substantially higher than the overall company attrition rate of 16.1%, suggesting that the combination of overtime and early tenure may represent a significant employee retention risk.

## Business Implications

The analysis suggests that employee retention strategies should prioritize:

- Employees who frequently work overtime
- Sales Representatives and other high-attrition job roles
- Employees within their first four years at the company
- Lower-income employee groups
- Early-tenure employees who also work overtime

Reducing excessive overtime, improving early-career employee support, and reviewing compensation and retention strategies for high-risk roles may help reduce employee turnover.
